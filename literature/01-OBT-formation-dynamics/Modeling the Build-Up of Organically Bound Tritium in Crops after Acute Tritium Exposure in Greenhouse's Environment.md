## ABSTRACT

The processes of tritium uptake, losses, transformation and organically bound tritium (OBT) build-up in crops such as potatoes, tomatoes, lettuce and wheat were studied in a set of experiments on crop short-term exposure to tritiated water (HTO) in a greenhouse. The crops were exposed at the stage of linear growth between blossoming and ripening. The results of experiments were used to develop a model of OBT build-up in crop's storage organs.

The model allows estimate tritium uptake, losses in crop's leaves as well as its transformation in OBT and transport to storage organs of plants. The model parameters were obtained from the measured data for each crop's type by using regression analysis. The model was validated against experiments. The model can be used to assess OBT activity in storage organs of crops upon short-term exposure in the atmosphere contaminated by HTO.

---

## I. INTRODUCTION

Crops are essential items of the human being's diet and organically bound tritium (OBT) can contribute to the internal irradiation dose due to ingestion. Hence, studying the build-up and behavior of OBT is a topical task. By now, a number of models are known to describe the processes of OBT build-up, losses and transformation in plants. However, the accuracy of assessment depends essentially on the available experimental data for particular species of plants. Taking into account the aforementioned, experimental and modeling studies are required to determine the parameters of tritium uptake for agricultural plants and thus to enhance the validity of the dose assessment.

The model presented in the paper was developed to estimate tritium uptake and losses in leaves as well as OBT formation and translocation to crop's storage organs. Experimental setup, exposure conditions and the results of experiments are presented in this issue.

## II. MODEL OF OBT BUILD-UP

### II.A. Model Description

#### II.A.1. Model of HTO vapor uptake by leaves

Taking into account the results of previously published studies on dynamics of tritium oxide (HTO) uptake and losses by plants it was assumed that the major contribution of HTO vapors uptake by crop's leaves is due to stomatal assimilation.

This assumption allows apply the following kinetic equation to describe the process of HTO uptake by crops:

$$C_{pl} = C_{\text{max}}(1 - e^{-\omega t}) \quad (1)$$

where $C_{pl}$ is HTO activity in plants at time $t$, $C_{\text{max}}$ is the activity accumulated in plants, $\omega$ is the rate constant.

HTO equilibrium activity in leaves upon the short-term exposure is derived assuming that HTO transport from roots to leaves is negligible:

$$C_{\text{max}} = A \cdot R \cdot C_{\text{av}} \quad (2)$$

where $C_{\text{max}}$ is HTO activity in plant leaves at equilibrium (Bq/mL); $A = 1.1$ is the ratio of tritium specific activity (T/H) in water to the specific activity in water vapor at equilibrium; $R$ is relative humidity; $C_{\text{av}}$ is HTO activity in air moisture (Bq/mL).

The rate constants in Eq. (1) were determined both for day- and nighttime exposure for each species of the crops.

#### II.A.2. Model of tritium loss by plants

In all the experiments on HTO losses by crop's leaves the following common features can be pointed out:

- fast initial drop of TFWT activity in leaves during the first hours as the result of stomatal assimilation and transpiration,
- the follow-up slow variation of TFWT activity resulted in several interrelated processes: HTO transport from the soil contaminated during the exposure, TFWT conversion to OBT and vice versa, tritiated matter transport to storage organs etc.

These assumptions are in a good agreement with the previously obtained results, where the rates of activity losses in leaves after the short-term exposure were also determined.

The equation describing the TFWT losses by leaves can be represented as a function with double exponent:

$$C_{pl}(t) = C_1 e^{-k_1 t} + C_2 e^{-k_2 t} + C_3 \quad (3)$$

where $C_{pl}$ is the TFWT activity in a plant, $C_1, C_2, C_3$ are fitting coefficients, $k_1, k_2$ are the rate constants of TFWT losses for the fast and slow process components, respectively.

Modeling results have shown that the rate of TFWT losses by plants due to stomatal assimilation exceeds the rates of transport processes in phloem (conducting tissue of plants) by two orders of magnitude.

#### II.A.3. The growth model of crop's storage organs

Among variety of quantitative models of plant's growth, the logistic function is extensively used:

$$N(t) = \frac{N_{\text{sat}}}{1 + \Delta N e^{-\lambda t}} \quad (4)$$

where $N(t)$ is the relative mass of an organ at the growth time $t$, $N_{\text{sat}}$ is the relative mass of an organ at ripening, $N_{\text{sat}} = 1$, $\lambda$ is the intrinsic rate of natural increase (day⁻¹), $\Delta N$ is the relative growth rate:

$$\Delta N = (N_{\text{sat}} - N_0) / N_0$$

where $N_0$ is the organ's mass at the time moment.

The growth function yields the fundamental parameters of growth such as the intrinsic rate of natural increase, $\lambda$ (day⁻¹) and the relative growth rate $\Delta N$.

For each of the studied crops both values have been obtained approximating the growth function (Eq. 4) to the experimental data.

#### II.A.4. Model of OBT build-up in crops

The model of OBT build-up was developed to assess OBT activity in a storage organ at harvest following the exposure at linear growth stage.

To estimate the rate of OBT build-up due to photosynthesis in the absence of losses the approach based on the crop's growth curve can be used. According to this approach OBT activity in a crop's storage organ is the weighted sum of the TFWT activity in leaves at different time moments of growth of a considered organ.

$$\frac{dC_{OBT}}{dt} = \alpha C_{HTO}(t) \frac{dN(t)}{dt} \quad (5)$$

where $C_{OBT}$ is tritium activity in combustion water of a plant's organ sample at harvest (Bq/L), $C_{HTO}(t)$ is TFWT activity of leaves at time t (Bq/L), $dN(t)/dt$ is the variation rate of the relative mass of an organ; $\alpha$ is the fractional ratio determined as the ratio of OBT and TFWT specific activities.

The OBT build-up model has been validated against the experiments on OBT build-up in storage organs of tomato and potato crops upon the short-term HTO exposure in greenhouse performed in 2007 field season.

The modeling has been done based on Eqs. (1)-(5) and the kinetic constants needed for modeling purposes have been taken from Tables I-III. The deviations were obtained by introduction of 5% variation into kinetic rate constants.

Based on the validation results, the following conclusions can be made. The model described satisfactory the process of TFWT uptake and losses by tomato leaves: the error was within $\pm 8\%$. For the TFWT uptake in potato leaves, the experimental and modelling results essentially differed: in the daytime experiment the error was $\pm 100\%$. This variation can be explained by low activity values. Variation of the biomass of tomato fruits and potato tubers were predicted rather accurately; the average error is close to $\pm 6\%$. The increment of OBT activity in tomato fruits was determined with satisfactory accuracy $\pm 8\%$.

## IV. CONCLUSIONS

The paper presents the model of OBT build-up in crops. The model has been developed on the basis of results obtained in the greenhouse experiments on crops exposure to HTO vapours. The required rate constants for the model have been received from experimental data.

Tritium uptake and losses in leaves is described by the first-order kinetics equations. To model the dynamics of crop's biomass increase the function of logistic growth is used. OBT transport and build-up in the storage organ are calculated assuming that a constant fraction of OBT formed in leaves is transported to the storage organ. Regression analysis was used to obtain model parameters from measured data in 2005 and 2006 field experiments.

The obtained data have shown that tritium uptake and losses by plants correspond to the first-order kinetic equation. Such a behavior of TFWT uptake by plants is primarily due to gas-exchange through stomata and secondly - through epidermis.

The rate of OBT build-up was estimated basing the plant growth curve. The variation of OBT activity in the crop's storage organ is estimated on the TFWT activity in leaves at the current moment of time.

The rate constants resulting from the regression analysis allow assess the expected crops contamination in case of atmospheric HTO release if HTO activity and duration of exposure are known.

To validate the model there were predicted OBT and TFWT activities in tomato and potato crops for exposure experiments and satisfactory agreement was achieved.

Based on the modeling results, the following conclusions can be drawn:

- the model described satisfactory the process of TFWT uptake in tomato leaves;
- the calculated activities of TFWT in potato leaves at the end of the exposure turned out to be underestimated;
- the model predicted satisfactory the variation dynamics of the biomass increase of the tomato fruit and potato tubers;
- the calculated relative OBT increment in storage organs at harvest was tenth fractions of a percent.

These conclusions agree with the published results.
