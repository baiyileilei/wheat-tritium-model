# Evaluating the accumulation potential of organically bound tritium in edible seaweed under dark and light conditions through a deuterium tracer experiment

**Authors:** Yuhi Satoh, Takashi Tani
**Journal:** Marine Pollution Bulletin
**Year:** 2026
**Volume:** 222
**Article:** 118709

## Abstract
To estimate the accumulation potential of organically bound tritium (OBT) by seaweed from tritiated water in the coastal area beside Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant (FDNPP), the organically bound deuterium (OBD) accumulation in seaweed was examined in incubation experiments using artificial seawater with addition of $^{2}\mathrm{H}_{2}\mathrm{O}$. In this experiment, two species (*Ulva prolifera*, *Gracilaria vermiculophylla*) were investigated at 15 and $25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ under dark and light, respectively. OBD accumulation rates were higher under light than dark. OBD accumulation rates were higher at $25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ or under light in *U. prolifera*, while these trends were not seen in *G. vermiculophylla*. Using the obtained data, a maximum OBT concentration in seaweed was calculated from seawater with upper permissible tritium concentration from FDNPP, as proposed by TEPCO. OBT result was $0.11 \mathrm{Bq~g^{-1}}$ per wet weight, far smaller than the upper limit value in foods ($10 \mathrm{Bq~g^{-1}}$) proposed by WHO/FAO.

## 1. Introduction
Since August 2023, treated water containing controlled tritium ($^{3}\mathrm{H}$, T) concentrations has been released to the coastal ocean near Fukushima on a plan developed by TEPCO. The upper permissible limit of tritium concentration in the treated water for the release is $1500 \mathrm{Bq} \mathrm{L}^{-1}$.

Seaweeds were not evaluated in previous predictive estimations. This organism is the most productive primary producer in coastal oceans (Pessarrodona et al., 2022) and is a common fishery product worldwide (Buschmann et al., 2017). Therefore, the accumulation potential of OBT from HTO in seawater in seaweeds should be understood.

## 2. Materials and methods

### 2.1. Seaweed sampling and incubation experiments
The targeted seaweeds (*U. prolifera* and *G. vermiculophylla*) were sampled in a river mouth connected to the Pacific Ocean in northeast Japan. Seaweeds (approximately $100 \mathrm{g}$ wet weight for each species) were transferred to an incubation container containing $1 \mathrm{L}$ of artificial seawater. After $10 \mathrm{mL}$ of $\mathrm{D}_{2}\mathrm{O}$ addition ($99.9\%$ of $^{2}\mathrm{H}$), approximately $10 \mathrm{g}$ of seaweed samples were recovered on days 0.5, 1, 2, 3, 6, and 7.

### 2.2. Analysis
Both total and NE-OBDs were measured in the seaweed sample using an isotope ratio mass spectrometer (Isoprime 100).

### 2.3. Calculations of OBD accumulation rates and saturation levels
OBD accumulation rates were calculated by fitting to a linear equation:

$$
C_{\mathrm{OBD}} = C_{\mathrm{OBD-I}} + at \quad (1)
$$

OBD saturation levels were calculated using:

$$
C_{\mathrm{OBD}} = C_{\mathrm{OBD-I}} + b[1 - \exp(-tc)] \quad (2)
$$

### 2.4. Estimation of the maximum OBT accumulation potential in seaweed
The saturated OBT concentration was calculated based on seawater containing the upper permissible HTO concentration ($1500 \mathrm{Bq} \mathrm{L}^{-1}$) as follows:

$$
C_{\mathrm{OBT-max}} = C_{\mathrm{W-HTO}} \times \frac{C_{\mathrm{OBD-max}}}{C_{\mathrm{W-2DH}}} \times \frac{100 - D}{100} \times S \quad (3)
$$

where $C_{\mathrm{OBT-max}}$ is the saturated OBT concentration in seaweed as wet weight ($\mathrm{Bq} \mathrm{g}^{-1}$ wet weight).

### 3.3. OBT accumulation potential in seaweed in seawater around Fukushima
The maximum OBT concentration in seaweed in seawater containing the upper permissible HTO concentration ($1500 \mathrm{Bq} \mathrm{L}^{-1}$) was estimated using Eq. (3). Saturated OBD concentration was referred from the highest data for total OBD under light conditions at $25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for *U. prolifera* (4253 ppm). The $^{2}\mathrm{H}$ concentration in the seawater was set to 8623 ppm. The maximum estimated OBT concentration in seaweed was $0.11 \mathrm{Bq} \mathrm{g}^{-1}$ wet weight, which is substantially lower than the recommended tritium levels in food outlined by FAO/WHO of less than $10 \mathrm{Bq} \mathrm{g}^{-1}$ wet weight.

## 4. Conclusion
The present study conducted an incubation experiment using a $\mathrm{D}_{2}\mathrm{O}$ tracer to evaluate OBT accumulation potential in seaweed within seawater near Fukushima, under conditions involving the release of treated water. As a result, the following were obtained:

1. OBT accumulation rates and saturation levels are influenced by water temperature, light conditions, chemical speciation, and seaweed species.
2. OBT accumulation rates under dark conditions were approximately half of those observed under light conditions.
3. Productivity and respiration rates were key factors affecting variations in OBT accumulation rates and saturation levels.
4. OBT accumulation in seaweed from HTO, derived from treated water in seawater near Fukushima, is predicted to be minimal.
